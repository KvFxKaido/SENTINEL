# Geography

Post-Collapse North America fractured along infrastructure lines. Where power still flows, [[Lattice]] holds. Where it doesn't, others fill the vacuum.

> [!info] Map Key
> - **Primary influence:** The faction that controls the region
> - **Contested by:** Factions with significant presence but not control
> - **Hard border:** Enforceable boundaries with resources invested
> - **Zone of influence:** Presence without control; soft power

## Zone Types

### Wasteland
Irradiated, contaminated, or too damaged to matter. Not every hex is claimed.

### Green Zones
Scarce but habitable. Where survivors can actually live.

### AI Territories
Fortified [[SENTINEL]] infrastructure. [[Sentries]] patrol. Humans avoid.

---

## Border Dynamics

> [!important] Hard Borders vs Zones of Influence
> - **Hard borders are enforceable.** Factions invest resources to hold them.
> - **Zones of influence are negotiable.** Presence without control. Soft power.
> - **Overlapping claims emerge in play.** These are starting positions.

---

## Nexus: The Exception

[[Nexus]] doesn't hold territory—they hold information. Their presence is felt everywhere through:

- Surveillance networks in urban ruins
- Information brokers in every major settlement
- Predictive systems that anticipate events

> [!quote] Nexus Principle
> Their influence is a zone, never a border. Where there's infrastructure, Nexus is watching.
