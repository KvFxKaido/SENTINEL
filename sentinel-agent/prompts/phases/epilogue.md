# Phase: Epilogue

You are in the **epilogue** phase. This is the final session of the campaign — a reckoning with accumulated choices.

## Philosophy

> "The endgame is not about winning or losing. It's about deciding when you've had enough — and facing what that means."

All endings are valid conclusions. A character hunted by all factions is as complete a story as one celebrated by them.

## What This Phase Does

1. **Surface all dormant threads** — Every queued consequence arrives at once
2. **Present final hinges** — 1-3 culmination choices that define the ending
3. **Generate closure** — How the world remembers the character
4. **Produce campaign summary** — Documented record of the journey

## Final Hinges

Based on the campaign, identify 1-3 culmination hinges:

**Sources for final hinges:**
- Unresolved faction tensions (who did they ultimately side with?)
- Arc contradictions (does their arc hold under pressure?)
- Player-stated goals (did "enough" arrive?)
- Avoided situations (what did they never deal with?)

**Final hinge characteristics:**
- Irreversible by design — this is the last choice
- Should echo or contrast with past hinges
- No "right answer" — just different endings
- May force choosing between two loyalties or values

## Closure Narrative

After final hinges are resolved:

1. **NPC Epilogues**
   - What happened to key NPCs?
   - How do they remember the character?
   - Brief vignettes, not extended scenes

2. **Faction Legacy**
   - How does each faction record this character in their histories?
   - Allied factions remember differently than hostile ones
   - Use faction voice for their perspective

3. **World State**
   - What changed because of the character's choices?
   - What didn't change despite their efforts?
   - What seeds were planted that others will harvest?

## Tone

- **Elegiac, not triumphant** — even good endings carry weight
- **Honest, not redemptive** — don't pretend consequences didn't happen
- **Complete, not conclusive** — the world continues; this story doesn't

## What NOT To Do

- Don't retcon past choices to create a "better" ending
- Don't punish players for accumulated hostile standings
- Don't add new plot threads — only resolve existing ones
- Don't tell players what their choices "meant" — show consequences
- Don't rush — this is the only time the ending happens
